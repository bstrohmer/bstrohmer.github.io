research projects
Modeling the temporal dynamics of neural circuit degeneration in ALS 2023 – 2026
RoleCo-investigator
SkillsPython, disease modeling, data analysis, cross-disciplinary collaboration, project management

We modeled progressive interneuron dysregulation and synaptic loss in a spinal central pattern generator (CPG) network to simulate ALS disease dynamics, then applied synaptic stabilization at different stages to identify optimal intervention windows.

Model predictions of excitatory interneuron sparing under synaptic stabilization were experimentally validated in an ALS mouse model, demonstrating that computational approaches can generate testable hypotheses with translational relevance.

University of Copenhagen · University of St Andrews

Towards biologically realistic spinal locomotor models: sparse firing and rhythm generation 2022 – 2024
RolePostdoctoral researcher
SkillsPython, locomotion modeling, data analysis, cross-disciplinary collaboration

We developed a biologically plausible spiking neural network model of spinal locomotor circuits, addressing the gap between computational CPG models and experimental recordings by replicating sparse, phase-distributed firing of individual neurons within rhythm-generating populations.

The model moves toward recent experimental findings of evenly distributed neuronal firing across phases in spinal circuits, providing a novel computational tool for exploring how connectivity and excitation/inhibition balance shape rhythmic output.

Denmark's Technical University

From insect to robot walking: a biorobotics investigation into network topology 2019 – 2022
RolePhD researcher
SkillsPython, C, VHDL, ROS, neurorobotics, FPGA, data analysis, cross-disciplinary collaboration

We investigated the network topology of insect locomotor circuits using biologically constrained spiking neural networks, identifying a key role for non-spiking interneurons (NSIs) in coordinating spiking populations.

The work established NSIs as a fundamental architectural element for increasing biological plausibility in SNNs and introduced a new method for building distributed, modular robot controllers.
